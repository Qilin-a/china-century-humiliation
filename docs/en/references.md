## Historiographical Notes

### Perspectives on the "Century of Humiliation"

The concept of the "Century of Humiliation" (百年国耻) is itself a historical construct that emerged in early 20th century China. Scholars have noted:

1. **Chinese Nationalist Perspective**: Emphasizes foreign aggression and Chinese victimization
2. **Western Revisionist Perspective**: Examines internal Chinese factors and agency
3. **Post-Colonial Perspective**: Analyzes power dynamics and imperialism
4. **Comparative Perspective**: Places Chinese experience in global context of colonialism

### Key Historiographical Debates

1. **Causes of Chinese Weakness**: Internal decay vs. external pressure
2. **Nature of Treaties**: Legal agreements vs. imposed diktat
3. **Chinese Agency**: Passive victims vs. active participants
4. **Modernization**: Westernization vs. selective adaptation
5. **Historical Memory**: Objective history vs. political narrative
